Pete's Little Lunch Box

Background

Pete's Little Lunch Box is a food truck on Drexel's campus that serves breakfast and lunch with a focus on fast comforting food.

The app prioritizes the user's needs with a streamlined ordering process to accommodate for busy students and workers.

Goals

  • Create a prototype of an app
  • Research the client and find out their needs
  • Research potential user base and analyze the user needs
  • From this information, come up with features that will help both the users and the client
  • Create an initial wireframe, then iterate on the ideas and features in it based on feedback from usability testing

Research

User Personas

The consumer base for Pete's Little Lunch Box largely consists of students, construction workers, and Drexel professors. All of these users need a quick and convenient option for meals on campus.

Journey Map

From our user research, we mapped out potential highs and lows of the app's critical path from an ideal user's standpoint.

Styles

Mood Board

From these mood boards, our group agreed that we would combine a graphic style with photorealism that would come from the images of the food. We were inspired by the existing menu design, but wanted to modernize their look.

Fonts & Colors

For all of the typography in our app, we used the font family 'Gilroy.' To introduce visual hierarchy, we used different weights of the font, as well as designated styles for call to actions within the app. The red and blue color palette is derived from the original colors of the truck, however, we made the tones slightly cooler for a more modern feel.

Critical Path

Placing an Order

The user wants to order a cheesesteak. They scroll through the menu and click on the lunch page. They order a cheesesteak and can customize their order on the 'Edit Item' page.

Checkout

The user adds their item to cart and returns to the menu page in case they want to order another item. When they are ready, the user clicks on the cart icon and reviews their cart. Then, they tap on the checkout button.

Login & Payment

When the user wants to checkout, they are taken to the login page where they have to sign in or make an account before proceeding with their order. After they login, they can add a new payment method with an option to save their payment for future use.

Tracking & Order Details

Once the payment method is added, the user reviews their pick up information and confirms their payment. After that, they are able to track the order's progress. Once the order is ready, the user can hit "View Order Details" and show the truck owners their reciept when they pick up their food.
