Pete's Little Lunchbox

Background

Pete's Little Lunch Box is a food truck on Drexel's campus that serves breakfast and lunch with a focus on fast comforting food.

The app prioritizes the user's needs with a streamlined ordering process to accommodate for busy students and workers.

Goals

  • Create a prototype of an app
  • Research the client and find out their needs
  • Research potential user base and analyze the user needs
  • From this information, come up with features that will help both the users and the client
  • Create an initial wireframe, then iterate on the ideas and features in it based on feedback from usability testing

Research

User Personas

The consumer base for Pete's Little Lunch Box largely consists of students, construction workers, and Drexel professors. All of these users need a quick and convenient option for meals on campus.

Critical Path

The user logs into their account and wants to order a cheesesteak. They go to the scroll through the menu and clicks on the lunch page.

The user selects the cheesesteak menu item. They customize it the way they want to and add the item to their cart.

The user clicks on the cart icon and reviews their cart. They proceed to the checkout and add a payment method.

Once the payment method is added, they pay for their order and are able to track its progress. They hit "View Order Details" and show the truck owners their reciept when they pick up their food.
